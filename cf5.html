JOB SEARCH TECHNIQUES



India's demographic bulge or the younger generation is in danger of sliding into a lopsided paunch and weigh the nation down, crimping its gross domestic product. The problem is simple: Indians are obsessed with textbook education and whitecollar dreams, proving to be misfits in the corporate setup.

To help map this crisis and work towards building a pool of skilled resources, industry experts shared their insights at the Hindustan Times Shine HR Conclave – The Punjab Chapter, presented by Lovely Professional University at its campus.

After a speech by Ashok Kumar Mittal, founder chancellor of Lovely Professional University, Poonam Bhatia, senior partner, Search Value Consultants, the moderator for the session, kicked off discussiond by asking the panelists, “What’s at the root of the skill gap and what should be done to overcome the crisis?”

Deepak Jalan, head-RMG, Tech Mahindra, was of the opinion that “it is entirely a domino effect and it all starts in a child’s family and classroom. The youth should, therefore, be guided in the right direction from the beginning.” According to Abhay Kapoor, AVP-HR, Escorts, “Training and development of human capital should be given utmost importance.”


Arun David, VP-HR; VE Commercial Vehicles was of the opinion that “There should be a close-knit mechanism between the industry and the academia to make the youth aware of the industry protocols.” The remedy? Industry exposure through on-the-job training can effectively de-root the skill crisis, he added.
